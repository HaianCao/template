HALL OF GLORY
Cao Hai An

One significant advantage of early preschool enrollment is the fostering of social and cognitive development. Interacting with peers and educators in a structured environment enhances social skills and cultivates a foundation for learning. For instance, children exposed to early education often exhibit improved communication abilities and enhanced emotional intelligence, aiding their adaptability in diverse social settings. Additionally, preschools provide a structured curriculum that encourages cognitive development through activities promoting language, numeracy, and problem-solving skills.
Tran Duy Anh

However, a potential drawback of early preschool attendance revolves around the risk of excessive academic pressure. Some children might find themselves subjected to an overly structured environment that may impede their natural curiosity and creativity. For instance, the emphasis on academic readiness at a very young age might inadvertently lead to stress and anxiety in children, impacting their mental well-being. This pressure in school may have a severe detrimental effect on the natural growth of a child. Moreover, prolonged hours in a structured setting could limit opportunities for free play and exploration, which are crucial for holistic development.
Tran Duy Anh
